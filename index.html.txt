<h2>Introduction</h2>

<p>

South Korea's shipbuilding industry is a global leader in constructing
high-value vessels including LNG carriers, ultra-large container ships,
offshore platforms, and increasingly green and autonomous ships.

Its success is built on advanced engineering expertise, integrated
industrial clusters, world-class manufacturing, and continuous
investment in research and development.

This page highlights Korea's shipbuilding background and explores
practical ways its experience and technology can support
<strong>MASGA (Make American Shipbuilding Great Again)</strong>.

</p>

</div>

</section>

<section id="background">

<div class="card">

<h2>Background</h2>

<p>

Korea's emergence as a global shipbuilding powerhouse began through
strategic industrial policies and sustained investments from the
1970s onward.

Major shipbuilding hubs such as Ulsan, Geoje, and Mokpo created
highly efficient industrial clusters where shipyards, suppliers,
research institutes, and universities work closely together.

</p>

<br>

<h3>Key Strengths</h3>

<ul>

<li>Integrated industrial clusters</li>

<li>Advanced digital ship design and modular construction</li>

<li>Continuous investment in R&D and green technologies</li>

<li>Strong export competitiveness</li>

<li>Highly developed supplier ecosystem</li>

<li>Global leadership in LNG carriers and high-value vessels</li>

</ul>

</div>

</section>

<section id="help">

<div class="card">

<h2>How the Korean Shipbuilding Industry Can Help MASGA</h2>

<h3>1. Strategic Technology Partnerships</h3>

<p>
Develop bilateral R&D programs and joint ventures to transfer
advanced shipbuilding technologies and digital manufacturing methods.
</p>

<h3>2. Modular Construction</h3>

<p>
Introduce Korean modular block construction techniques into American
shipyards to improve productivity, quality, and efficiency.
</p>

<h3>3. Supplier Development</h3>

<p>
Strengthen U.S. marine suppliers through joint procurement,
matching grants, and long-term purchasing commitments.
</p>

<h3>4. Workforce Development</h3>

<p>
Create exchange programs and establish joint training centers
to transfer practical shipbuilding skills and production methods.
</p>

<h3>5. Green Fleet Transition</h3>

<p>
Cooperate on LNG, methanol, ammonia-ready, and hybrid-electric
vessel technologies to support sustainable shipping.
</p>

<h3>6. Industrial Policy</h3>

<p>
Adapt successful Korean financing, industrial clustering,
and procurement models to strengthen America's shipbuilding
ecosystem.

</p>

<div class="note">

<strong>Implementation Note</strong><br><br>

Partnerships should strengthen the U.S. industrial base while
encouraging technology transfer, workforce development,
local manufacturing, and intellectual property protection.

</div>

</div>

</section>
